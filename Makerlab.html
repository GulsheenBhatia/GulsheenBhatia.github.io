MAKER LAB 1

Colour Box

Summary

When designing and creating different projects sometimes picking out colours and looking for inspiration is difficult but the colour box aids helping us better visualize the colour. Rather than picking and choosing different colours in the design to see what works well with one another you will be able to interact with the physical colours to pick and choose the colour you decide. You can change out different colours to figure out which work well together. This concept was created based off of the online version of colour schemes. Making the online version physical allows you to see an assortment of different colours rather than just a select few at a time. Seeing things in person allows designers to visualize what it will look like once it is finished.

Description

When thinking about designing and making the prototype we were just going to put paper as shades but that didn't work as it was not sustainable and not visible from the top so we decided to use cardboard. For me the biggest challenge was to make the colours print in perfect size that is 8.5 X 6.5 so it would be all the same size. When we started to make a prototype we had two designs in mind, It took some tinkering with this approach to realize which method would be most effective.

Review

PROTOTYPE PRODUCTION METHOD DETAILS
Materials needed:
- Cardboard Box (10 in x 12 in x 7 in)
- Smaller Boxes Inside
- Variety of Different Colours
- Removable Colour Swatches
- Customizable
Steps:
1. For making the colour box first we found the most used colours and printed them in the size 8.5 X 6.5.
2. Then we cut a thin cardboard into 5.5 X 7.5 cm and cover the cardboard with the coloured paper we printed.
3. We use a big cardboard box and add the pieces inside in different colour groups
This was time consuming but worked perfectly with the idea we had in mind. In the end we had 133 different colours to allow users to have a variety of different colours to choose from which could be removed and moved around depending on your design choices.

Concluding Evaluation

After presenting our prototype we discussed our current design limitations. Firstly, the prototype we made is not of the best quality, it is not durable enough. If in the future we have to make a better prototype we will not use print paper and paste on cardboards. Also the design right now contains limited colours, we would add more colours in future. We would make the box more efficient and easy to carry around while traveling by adding a handle and using a box made with bioplastics. Overall, we would like to make our prototype better in the future and also incorporate new ideas.
